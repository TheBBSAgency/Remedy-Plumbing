Replace the anode rod every 2–3 years. It's a metal rod inside the tank that draws mineral impurities away from the tank wall. When it wears out and nobody replaces it, those impurities attack the tank instead. You can buy one at Menards for around $40. It's a simple job that most homeowners can do themselves.

Disconnect your outside hoses every fall. Seasonal & Preventive Plumbing

Two things matter most: disconnect all outside hoses from your hose bibs, and remove the black corrugated discharge hose from your sump pump. That hose will freeze in winter and prevent the pump from draining — burning it out. Also check your basement sills for drafts near water lines and seal them up.
At least once a year — ideally before snowmelt season in late winter or early spring. Pour a bucket of water into the pit to confirm the float switch triggers and the pump runs. If it doesn't, call us.
